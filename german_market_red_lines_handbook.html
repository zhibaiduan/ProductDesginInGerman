Germany has 17 independent data protection authorities — the most aggressive enforcement landscape in Europe. The combination of GDPR, BDSG, and TDDDG creates overlapping obligations where a single product decision can trigger simultaneous violations. German DPAs issued €46.9M in fines in 2025 alone, led by a €45M penalty against Vodafone.
RED LINE 01
Processing personal data without a valid legal basis
Data & Privacy
What is prohibited
Collecting, storing, or using personal data without one of the six GDPR legal bases: consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, or legitimate interest. Advertising profiling cannot rely on "legitimate interest" — German DPAs consistently reject this. Hannoversche Volksbank was fined €900K for exactly this: using customer data for digital affinity scoring under a "legitimate interest" claim.

RED LINE 02
Placing non-essential cookies or trackers without prior opt-in consent
Data & Privacy
What is prohibited
Loading analytics, advertising, or tracking scripts before the user gives explicit opt-in consent. This includes Google Tag Manager itself — the VG Hannover ruled in March 2025 that GTM loading before consent violates TDDDG §25. A "Reject all" button must be equally prominent as "Accept all." RED LINE 04
AI emotion recognition in workplaces or educational institutions
EU AI Act
What is prohibited
Any AI system that infers the emotional states of employees or students via cameras, voice analysis, biometric signals, or physiological data — regardless of stated purpose. Banned applications include: call centre stress-tracking tools, recruitment video interview emotion scoring, student attention inference from webcam feeds, online proctoring emotional flagging, and HR dashboards aggregating "mood signals." The narrow exception is genuine medical or safety-critical use only.

RED LINE 05
AI social scoring systems — rating people based on behaviour or personal characteristics
EU AI Act
What is prohibited
AI systems that evaluate or classify individuals based on social behaviour or personal characteristics and use that classification to cause detrimental treatment in unrelated contexts, or treatment disproportionate to the original behaviour. Prohibited examples: a rental platform assigning a "trust score" from social media activity to deny housing; an employer rating "cultural fit" from online behaviour history; a credit product using personality trait inference. Applies to both public and private entities.

SCHUFA Holding · CJEU C-634/21 · December 2023
Landmark ruling: SCHUFA's automated credit scores constitute "automated individual decision-making" under GDPR Art. 22 — even though the bank makes the final lending decision — because the score is "determinative in practice." Combined with AI Act Art. 5(1)(c), any scoring system that classifies people and influences consequential decisions is now under dual-regulation scrutiny.
RED LINE 06
Scraping faces from the internet to build biometric databases
EU AI Act
What is prohibited
Creating or expanding facial recognition databases by indiscriminately scraping facial images from the internet or CCTV footage. This is Clearview AI's core business model — and it is an absolute prohibition under EU AI Act Art. 5(1)(e). Also prohibited: real-time remote biometric identification in public spaces for law enforcement (Art. 5(1)(h)), except in narrowly defined emergencies with prior judicial authorisation.

BaFin is one of the most aggressive financial regulators in Europe. Operating financial services without a licence is not a civil matter in Germany — it is a criminal offence carrying up to 5 years imprisonment. In 2025 alone, BaFin issued its largest-ever AML fine (€45M against J.P. Morgan SE) and banned a crypto product within weeks of launch.
RED LINE 07
Embedding payment or lending features without a BaFin licence
Financial Services
What is prohibited
Operating payment services, deposit-taking, credit provision, investment advice, or crypto-asset services in Germany without the appropriate BaFin authorisation. BaFin applies substance-over-form: even if you call your product a "marketplace tool" or "financial wellness feature," if it moves money, holds deposits, or provides credit decisions, a licence is required. Embedding a licensed partner's product does not automatically exempt you — BaFin assesses who controls the customer relationship.

Germany has the world's most advanced digital health framework — and the world's strictest software-as-medical-device enforcement. A single word in your marketing copy can reclassify your app as a medical device overnight. The criminal penalties under MPDG §92 reach 10 years imprisonment for commercial violations endangering public health.
RED LINE 08
Marketing software as "diagnostic" or "therapeutic" without CE certification
Medical & Health
What is prohibited
Placing software on the German market that claims to diagnose, monitor, predict, treat, or alleviate medical conditions without CE marking as a medical device (minimum Class IIa under MDR Rule 11). The trigger is the intended purpose communicated to users — a single marketing claim like "helps manage your diabetes" or "detects early signs of depression" reclassifies the app. Landing page copy, app store descriptions, and customer support scripts all count as "intended purpose" evidence.

Ada Health · navigated correctly
Ada Health obtained CE Class IIa medical device certification for its clinical-facing product while keeping its consumer app explicitly positioned as a "health guide, not a diagnostic tool." The dual product strategy — regulated clinical tool + non-medical consumer app — is the correct structural response to Germany's strict intended-purpose doctrine. Companies that blur this line face enforcement action.
Germany's works council system (Betriebsrat) gives employee representatives mandatory co-determination rights over any technology that can monitor employee behaviour. Deploying a tool without Betriebsrat consent doesn't just risk fines — the measure is automatically legally void. This applies even if monitoring isn't the tool's primary purpose.
RED LINE 09
Deploying employee monitoring tools without Betriebsrat written consent
Labor & HR
What is prohibited
Using any technical system that is objectively capable of monitoring employee behaviour or performance without prior Betriebsrat agreement (Betriebsvereinbarung). The BAG's 1975 "objective suitability" standard applies: actual monitoring intent is irrelevant — if the tool can monitor, consent is required. This covers: productivity tracking software, login/logout logging, email metadata analysis, GPS in company vehicles, Microsoft Viva Insights, AI-assisted performance dashboards, and ERP systems with detailed activity logs. BetrVG §95(2a) explicitly extends this to AI used for personnel selection criteria.

H&M · Hamburg DPA · €35.3M · 2020
H&M's Nuremberg service centre systematically recorded employees' personal lives — vacation details, illness symptoms, family problems, religious beliefs — in a network drive accessible to 50+ managers, used for personnel decisions. Germany's largest GDPR fine at the time. Core violation: secret, systematic recording of employee data beyond any employment purpose. Employee data must be purpose-limited, minimised, and accessible only on a need-to-know basis.
Germany's consumer protection framework combines EU-level rules with German-specific requirements that regularly surprise international software companies. Missing a cancellation button makes your subscription terminable at will. Missing an Impressum triggers immediate legal warning letters. These are active, frequently enforced obligations — not theoretical risks.
RED LINE 10
No cancellation button for subscription contracts (Kündigungsbutton)
Consumer Protection
What is prohibited
Any B2C subscription website that does not provide a permanently visible, two-click-accessible cancellation button labelled exactly "Jetzt kündigen" or equivalent. OLG Köln ruled in 2025 that a mandatory login before seeing the cancellation button is illegal. BGH ruled in May 2025 that even one-time contracts with automatic renewal require the button.

RED LINE 11
Missing or incomplete Impressum (legal notice)
Consumer Protection
What is prohibited
Operating a commercially active website in Germany without a complete Impressum accessible within two clicks from any page. Required fields: complete legal entity name, registered address (no PO boxes), email, phone number, legal form, registration court and number, VAT ID, named responsible person. TMG was replaced by DDG in May 2024 — many existing Impressums reference the wrong law and are technically non-compliant.

Industry-wide · Abmahnindustrie
Germany has a well-established "Abmahnindustrie" — law firms that systematically scan websites for Impressum deficiencies and issue paid warning letters. Missing or incomplete Impressums are the single most common basis for commercial warning letters in German e-commerce. International companies launching German market operations routinely receive 2–5 Abmahnungen in their first year for Impressum issues alone.
